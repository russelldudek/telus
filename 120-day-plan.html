120-Day Entry Plan

Managing Director, CX AI Delivery | Portfolio diagnosis, lighthouse proofs and practice operating cadence

Days 0-30

Read the system

  • Review practice P&L, pipeline, backlog, utilization, delivery health, partner economics and managed-account growth plans.
  • Interview executive sponsors, Business Development, Digital Solutions, CXM account leaders, operations, finance, product, engineering, security, legal and talent leaders.
  • Shadow agents and frontline leaders in representative queues; observe work, exceptions, coaching, knowledge and failure demand.
  • Review ten wins, ten misses and ten stalled or under-optimized deployments.
  • Map where decisions split across sales, solutioning, implementation, adoption and managed operations.

Output: practice system map, P&L hypothesis, account segmentation and decision-bottleneck register.

Days 31-60

Choose the portfolio

  • Segment existing and prospective accounts by outcome potential, interaction profile, data/knowledge readiness, authority risk and commercial path.
  • Select two lighthouse transformations: one managed-account expansion and one new-market or partner-led offer proof.
  • Identify stop/hold candidates consuming scarce practice capacity without a strong value path.
  • Define the workshare, human authority, platform/partner/IP composition, success evidence and change burden for each lighthouse.
  • Align named executive sponsors and operating owners.

Output: portfolio investment decisions, lighthouse charters and first version of the CX AI Workshare Map.

Proof, Industrialize and Scale

Days 61-90

Run the proof

  • Launch two bounded transformations with visible outcome, authority, evidence and production-ownership contracts.
  • Use forward-deployed teams to work shoulder-to-shoulder with agents, supervisors and operations leaders.
  • Instrument adoption, override, exception, service quality, delivery cost and client-outcome signals.
  • Resolve scope and ownership ambiguity before it becomes margin leakage.
  • Capture reusable decisions, components, estimation assumptions and change patterns as the work happens.

Output: live outcome review, delivery evidence, corrected economics and reusable pattern candidates.

Days 91-120

Industrialize the advantage

  • Convert validated patterns into offer modules, estimation logic, governance standards, partner plays and enablement.
  • Set balanced practice scorecard spanning growth, margin, adoption, quality, delivery cycle, reuse and capability.
  • Define talent architecture across consulting, CX architecture, data, AI, integration, change, operations and partner specialties.
  • Publish the next two-quarter portfolio and capacity plan.
  • Present explicit invest, sequence, partner and stop decisions to executive leadership.

Output: CX AI practice operating system and next-wave growth portfolio.

Hypotheses to validate, not assumptions to impose

  • The largest near-term growth pool may be managed-account transformation, where TELUS Digital already controls enough operating context to prove value.
  • The differentiator may be the combination of operator learning, forward-deployed engineering, Fuel iX and broad platform partnerships - not any single product.
  • The practice may need stronger shared economics and adoption governance across presales, delivery and operations.

Failure modes and countermeasures

Failure modeCountermeasure

Pilots persist without an economic path.Use explicit invest, sequence, partner and stop decisions at portfolio review.
