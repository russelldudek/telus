Actual mandate

Build a profitable transformation practice, not a catalog of AI features.

Turn the operating knowledge of a global CX business into offers that win, deliver, improve managed accounts and become more valuable each time they are used.

From

Operating contradictions

The job lives in the tension between scale and judgment.

The leader cannot resolve these tensions by choosing one side. The operating model has to make the tradeoffs visible, governable and commercially useful.

Transform labor economics
AND
Protect service continuity

Managed accounts cannot become experimentation zones; the transformation must fund itself without destabilizing customer commitments.

Standardize the offer
AND
Respect the client stack

Repeatability comes from decisions, evidence and reusable components - not forcing every client into the same architecture.

Expand autonomy
AND
Preserve human authority

Agentic CX needs explicit exception rights, escalation ownership and quality evidence, especially in regulated or consequential journeys.

Sell transformation
AND
Own delivery reality

The P&L is exposed when presales optimism, implementation scope, frontline adoption and managed operations are governed separately.

Partner broadly
AND
Differentiate clearly

The ecosystem creates reach; TELUS Digital's operating scale, forward-deployed learning and Humanity-in-the-Loop posture create the defensible advantage.

Start with demand, failure modes, customer intent, agent work and operational evidence - not an AI product demo.

Harvest the reusable advantage

Turn learning into offer design, estimation, partner plays, enablement, governance patterns and managed-service growth.

First 120 days

Enter as a portfolio operator, not a tourist with a framework.

The first objective is to learn where TELUS Digital already wins, where delivery economics leak, and where the strongest account transformations can create both client value and reusable practice advantage.

Days 0-30

Read the system

  • Map practice P&L, pipeline, offers, delivery portfolio and partner motions.
  • Shadow agents, operations leaders, solution teams and account executives.
  • Review wins, misses, stalled pilots, margin leakage and adoption evidence.
  • Identify decision bottlenecks, not just capability gaps.
Days 31-60

Choose the portfolio

  • Segment managed accounts and new-market opportunities by value and readiness.
  • Select lighthouse transformations and explicit stop/hold candidates.
  • Define workshare, authority, evidence and commercial thesis for each.
  • Align partner and proprietary roles.
Days 61-90

Run the proof

  • Launch two bounded cross-functional transformations.
  • Establish weekly outcome, adoption, delivery and economics review.
  • Remove scope ambiguity and escalate production ownership early.
  • Capture reusable patterns as delivery occurs.
Days 91-120

Industrialize the advantage

  • Convert evidence into offer, estimation, governance and partner playbooks.
  • Set practice scorecard and portfolio cadence.
  • Publish talent and capability plan.
  • Present invest, sequence, partner and stop decisions to the executive team.

Hard objection
"He has not been a Partner in a top-tier CX consultancy or run a global contact-center AI P&L."

That is the strongest reasonable concern, and I would not disguise it. My case is adjacent: I have built operating mechanisms where technology, service, quality, people, economics and high-consequence execution meet. I have led AI-first operational change, scaled 24/7 customer-backward operations, managed complex technical delivery and designed governance around emerging technology.

The interview should test whether that operating judgment transfers to TELUS Digital's CX AI practice. I would welcome a bounded case: choose an account, expose the workshare and economics, define the authority boundary, and show what I would invest in, sequence, partner or stop.

Executive discovery

Questions that reveal the real practice mandate.

Where does the current CX AI P&L make money, and where does delivery or adoption quietly destroy margin?

Which managed accounts are structurally ready to shift from labor-based service to AI-enabled outcome models?

What has TELUS Digital learned as its own "Day 0 client" that is genuinely differentiated and repeatable?

Which decisions are currently unclear across Business Development, Digital Solutions, CXM account leadership and operations?

Where do platform partnerships accelerate the offer, and where must TELUS Digital own the operating IP?

What would make the board say, twelve months from now, that the practice changed the company's growth and margin trajectory?
