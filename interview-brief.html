The CX AI Workshare Map

A candidate-created operating model for account, delivery and P&L decisions.

Three decision lenses

1. Interaction workshare

Segment by complexity, risk, value, failure mode and customer intent. Assign Human-led, Assistive, Conversational or Agentic work intentionally.

2. Delivery composition

Connect platform, CRM, data, workflow, knowledge, governance, adoption and production ownership into one accountable path.

3. Practice economics

Review client outcome, service quality, adoption, delivery cost, margin, reuse and risk in the same portfolio decision.

4. Human authority boundary

Specify where AI prepares, recommends, converses, transacts or acts, and where human judgment remains mandatory.

Operating sequence

MoveDecisionFailure prevented
Read the interaction portfolioWhere is the work, value, risk and failure demand?Technology-first scope.
Set the authority boundaryWhat may AI do, and what remains human?Hidden autonomy and weak escalation.
Compose the delivery pathWhich platform, partner, IP and operating change fit?Tool sprawl and custom integration debt.
Prove service and economicsWhat evidence earns expansion?Go-live celebrated without realized value.
Harvest reusable advantageWhat becomes an offer, pattern or partner play?Learning trapped inside one account.

Core operating tensions

  • Transform labor economics while protecting service continuity.
  • Standardize offers while respecting client stacks and operating realities.
  • Expand autonomy while preserving explicit human authority.
  • Sell transformation while owning delivery and adoption reality.
  • Partner broadly while differentiating through operator scale, forward-deployed learning and responsible work design.

"Russell has not been a Partner in a top-tier CX consultancy or run a global contact-center AI P&L."

Answer: Correct. Do not hide the adjacency. Test the operating judgment. The evidence supports AI-enabled operations, scaled service execution, high-reliability technical delivery, governance, telemetry, adoption mechanisms and cross-functional accountability. The remaining gaps are CX-platform depth, global practice economics and formal consulting-partner tenure.

Discovery posture: Pair early with platform and finance leaders; learn the current P&L and delivery portfolio; use bounded account work to prove judgment; never claim specialist depth that belongs to the team.

Entry Logic and Executive Questions

120-day entry logic

Days 0-30 - Read

Map P&L, pipeline, offers, delivery portfolio, partner motions, wins, misses, stalled pilots, margin leakage and decision bottlenecks.

Days 31-60 - Choose

Segment accounts, select lighthouse transformations and stop/hold candidates, define workshare, authority, evidence and commercial thesis.

Days 61-90 - Prove

Launch two bounded transformations, run a weekly outcome/adoption/economics review and capture reusable patterns during delivery.

Days 91-120 - Industrialize

Convert evidence into offer, estimation, governance, partner and talent playbooks; establish practice scorecard and portfolio cadence.

Executive questions

  1. Where does the current CX AI P&L make money, and where does delivery or adoption quietly destroy margin?
  2. Which managed accounts are structurally ready to shift from labor-based service to AI-enabled outcome models?
  3. What has TELUS Digital learned as its own "Day 0 client" that is genuinely differentiated and repeatable?
  4. Which decisions are unclear across Business Development, Digital Solutions, account leadership and operations?
  5. Where do partnerships accelerate the offer, and where must TELUS Digital own the operating IP?
  6. What twelve-month result would prove this practice changed TELUS Digital's growth and margin trajectory?

90-second opening

TELUS Digital already has the ingredients of a differentiated CX AI practice. The opportunity is to make them compound. I would start by treating each target account as an interaction portfolio: define the human-AI workshare, authority boundary, delivery composition, evidence standard and economic thesis. Then I would use two lighthouse transformations to prove value and harvest reusable offer, partner and operating leverage.

What I would not do

  • Lead with a platform demo before reading the work and outcome.
  • Promise labor reduction without service, adoption and change evidence.
  • Claim specialist platform expertise that properly belongs to the team.
  • Scale autonomy without clear human authority, exception control and production ownership.
  • Protect weak pilots from a stop decision because they are politically visible.
